Understanding the Problem

Our team identified critical mental health challenges affecting college students. Below, we highlight the key issues that drive our mission to create impactful solutions.

Prevalence of Mental Health Issues

Recent studies show that over 60% of college students report struggling with mental health challenges, such as anxiety and depression. These issues significantly impact academic performance and overall well-being.

Lack of Accessible Resources

Many students are unaware of available mental health resources or find it challenging to access help due to stigma, financial constraints, or logistical barriers.

Social Isolation

Social isolation, amplified by the pandemic and increased reliance on virtual platforms, has left students feeling disconnected and unsupported.

Academic Stress

The pressure to excel academically often leads to burnout, decreased productivity, and adverse effects on mental health.

Stigma Around Mental Health

Students often hesitate to seek help due to stigma and fear of judgment, which worsens mental health conditions and prevents timely interventions.

Limited Peer Support

Lack of peer support or communities focused on mental well-being leaves many students feeling alone in their struggles.
